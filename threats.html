Phishing

  • Tricks individuals into providing sensitive information.
  • Appears as trustworthy entity in communications.
  • Often comes in form of emails, messages, or websites.

Malware

  • Includes viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, and adware.
  • Can steal, encrypt, or delete sensitive data.
  • Monitors users' computer activity without permission.

Ransomware

  • Encrypts a victim's files.
  • Attacker demands ransom to restore access.
  • Instructions on how to pay ransom and obtain decryption key.

Social Engineering

  • Manipulates individuals into divulging confidential information.
  • Includes phishing, pretexting, and baiting.
  • Relies on human interaction and psychological manipulation.

Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks

  • Aims to shut down a machine or network.
  • Makes target inaccessible to its intended users.
  • Accomplished by flooding target with traffic or triggering a crash.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks

  • Attacker intercepts and relays messages between two parties.
  • Allows attacker to steal sensitive information.
  • Targets login credentials, account details, and other personal info.

SQL Injection

  • Code injection technique that might destroy your database.
  • Common web hacking technique.
  • Malicious code in SQL statements via web page input.

Zero-Day Exploits

  • Targets vulnerabilities unknown to the vendor.
  • No patch or fix available for the vulnerability.
  • Particularly dangerous due to lack of awareness.
